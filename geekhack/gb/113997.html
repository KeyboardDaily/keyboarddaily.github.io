Hey everyone,
This is the Balance- an 1800 keyboard without an F-row and with a Knob.

One of my favorite keyboards to use is the Cypher - but when I use it, I really do miss having a knob to control volume and media. For that reason, even when doing tasks that are better suited for a numpad, I find myself using my Satisfaction75 quite frequently. This board was meant to fill that gap for me, while also playing with a new interesting side profile, considering two-tone color as a first-class design element, and integrating gasket mounting.
**Why is it called Balance?
**
There is a little accent piece on the back of the board, and when looking at the board from behind, to me, it looks like the whole width of the board is balancing on the accent piece.

**Supported Layouts:
**
PCB Support:

Most notable here is the exclusion of split right shift. On the PCB, we put the MCU there… so it would be a significant change to add split right shift support. Given this, and the lack of strong interest (less than 6% of submissions who would buy the board also wanted split right shift), we decided not to add it.
Universal ANSI plate:

A plate featuring support for all of these layouts is default.
NE Iso Avail?
We have not designed an ISO plate yet, but our plate files will be made publicly available as the GB comes close to ship time.
We aren’t planning on offering ISO plates with the GB.
Things I personally really Like about this Design:
Accent piece on top - this is not a functional pen rail, just an accent piece
Accent piece on the back - this is where the “Balance” name comes from
Knob - enough said
Side Profile - It looks great in 2 tones, and is kinda a “seamless” design

Design Features:

  • QMK and VIA Compatible PCB (Designed by Upas, assisted/routed by Ender)
  • ESD Protection
  • Centered USB-C C3 Unified Daughterboard
  • STM32 ARM Processor
  • No backlight LEDs, no hotswap
  • Poron Gasket Isolation Sandwich Mount
  • Custom soft rubber feet - these are bumpon compatible, so if you prefer bumpons, you can source and use those instead
  • ALPS Encoder and Cannon Logo Knob in Aluminum
  • 6 case parts (not including knob)
  • Two tone design with really cool side profile (this is one of my favorites)
  • No extra weight - imo, the keyboard is heavy enough as-is, and sounds good
  • 5.5 degree typing angle
  • About 15.7mm Front Height
  • About 29.7mm Back Height
  • Fits in an approximate 407mm x 121mm x 30mm bounding box (releasing this info so you can figure out how to make an appropriate wrist rest or etc.)
  • Brass plate by default, FR4 and CF options likely

Internals:

Gasket strips are not shown, but it should be pretty clear where they go.
No case foam / plate foam necessary.
Colors:
We’re currently planning on running at least 4 of the following colors, if not all 5:
Navy/Silver (Silver Alu accent)
E-White/Gold (Brass Accent)
Grey/Lilac (Lilac Alu Accent)
Black/Silver (Silver Alu Accent)
**Can you change X, Y, or Z?
**At this point, no.
