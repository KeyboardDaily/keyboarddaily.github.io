GMK LAZURITE
An allusion to High Renaissance art and the value of the pigment ultramarine
GMK Lazurite takes inspiration from the mineral lazurite found in lapis lazuli, processed and turned into a pigment called ultramarine. It was used by High Renaissance painters for the color blue, and was even priced more than the value of gold.
