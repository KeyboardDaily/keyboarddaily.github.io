Uses
These covers would be great not only for protecting your primary keyboard, but also great for keeping your other keyboards on display and out of a dark box or closet. I think it is preferable to keep something you worked hard on or spent money on out in the open and on display! I will be setting up my own keyboard display table near my desk to keep my boards on display and available for quick change outs. This wasn’t possible before without the covers because I don’t want them getting dusty by having them exposed in the open. You could also customize these by putting stickers on them. Redbubble.com has a lot of great custom stickers available for many different interests and those would look great on these.
Availability
In this GB, there will be 50 60% covers available, 40 TKL covers available, and 10 Full-Size Covers available.
Design
We will be using this design for the covers in this GB. The actual cover will be thicker and may be a couple cms wider and longer. These pics are an example of the Full-Size.


This is a cover that is meant is sit over the entire keyboard, not just the keys. The sides are open and there is a cable slot in the back to allow your cable to pass-through. The slots on the 60% and Full-Size are extra long to accommodate for different cable positions. The slot on the TKL is similar, but not as long since most TKL keyboards have their cables located near the center. These covers are also about 1 cm shorter in the front which gives the cover an angled, sloping appearance. These covers are also thicker than what is already out there which makes them feel a bit more solid and of higher quality. Their general purpose is to act as a display case and protect your keyboard from dust when not in use.
