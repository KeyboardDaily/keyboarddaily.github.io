The art of painting 3D goldfish on resin was invented and developed by Japanese artist Riusuke Fukahori. The technique involves painting on layers of resin to create a 3D image to make the fish looks as lively as possible. This technique has been admired and learned across the world in recent years, including Vietnam.
A look into the process:
A base layer of resin is molded and after drying for 24 hours, the painting process starts with painting on layers of resin. After finishing painting on one layer, it takes approximately 5 hours for the resin to dry up so that we can paint on the next layer. Each fish takes about 4 to 5 layers of painting and to finish the product, it requires about 7 to 9 layers of resin. For this reason, one wrist rest of 3D painting in resin would take around one week from start to finish.
Some extra information: Since its painting on different layers, our artist needs to ensure the accuracy across layers of resin. This process demands not only technicality but also patience, attention to details and creativity to create one finished and polished product.
