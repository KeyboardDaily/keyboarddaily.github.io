Inspiration
This is my first keycap set that I’ve been set on making on and off over the last few months but I thought I’d make it my goal this year to create this dream into a real set!
Tea & Biscuits is a massive part of the British culture whether you like the drink/food or not! The inspiration ironically came from when I was sat of my sofa watching TV in the evening with a Tea in my hand and a plate of various Biscuits - this couldn’t be more of a British thing honestly! That being said, this is where the set idea came from. Another thing is that Cream/brown are 2 of my favourite colours and lucky these 2 interests go hand in hand with each other.
If you’d like to share your thoughts on my set please do fill out the Interest Check Form
UPDATE - 02/06
Hello everyone! Here with another big update.
The main obvious part of this update is that the manufacturer has been switched to ePBT, this was a big choice I made for the set and couldn’t be happier with it.
With this change I’m able to make the kits the way I envisioned from the start, that being said I’ve added an NorDeUK kit as well as 40’S/Ortho!!
The first 2 collaborations have been announced down below as well with a couple more to come.
Colours
There are many different shades of tea that can be made as it’s all to do with preference! These colours chosen are to reflect both tea and biscuits colours combined into one, I went with this choice as it goes with how I envision the set. The chart below is the what I used as a colour reference for the tea aspect so you can see the direction I am taking, it might not be everyones cup of tea (ha ha funny pun). The dark colour is based of the shades that people class as builders tea as well as the biscuit that Bourbons has, the lighter brown shade is the most common shade of tea I see wherever I go as well as combining it with the lighter brown shades of biscuits - and lastly, the cream can be seen in the centre of the biscuit custard creams for example (plus it just works with the set really well!). I hope you can see where I am going with the set and the future of it.
