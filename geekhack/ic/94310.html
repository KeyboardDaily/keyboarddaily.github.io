Updated Google Form interest check!
///NEWS - 9/24/18///
I’ve been in touch with SP a few times over the last couple of weeks, and have seen some pre-production shots, set is looking great (the alpha/modifiers contrast is exactly what I was after), and it’s currently on track to be released this week on PMK! It’s been a long ride.
///NEWS - 5/8/18///
I was in touch with SP throughout the course of the interest check and they have decided that they will be producing and stocking the set via PMK! As such set design is now finalised (barring any issues that arise). The set will be produced in PBT DSA dye-sub as was always the intention and tentatively you can expect it to hit the store late summer. It’s in the production queue as we speak.
I’ll be returning to this as time allows in the meantime but only do drum up some further interest and aid in the production process as need arises, updates on timing will certainly follow however. Thanks for all the support and interest everyone, coming soon to a board near you!

This is my first foray into GeekHack really, fair to mid term lurker here - some of you may know me from over posting 40% boards with DSA over on /r/mk, others may not know me at all, but I’m here to present DSA Alchemy!
I designed this set initially for selfish reasons (I’m a DSA fanatic and personally I love this colour-way) - however after an initial IC there was some interest in me progressing this in a more formal manner. Thus the IC is born!
The basic concept behind this was a muted tone set that can be used as a base for further customisation - the colours have been picked to accentuate any additional panache you’d like to add to the board by way of accents, artisans, cabling, cases - whatever you crazy kids are up to these days. I’m a big fan of subtlety in keycap design and you can likely see I’ve taken design cues from granite and canvas to name but a couple. A little spice is added by the themed novelties - but again - I err to a simplistic school of design, flavour in this case is added through the addition of the set logo and alchemical symbols - rather than over complicating the palette.
The intention is for this to be PMK PBT dye sub, though that is not set in stone, I’ve emailed PMK to see if they would expect any issues with this (the legends on the accents showing some colour inconsistency is my only worry there) and am awaiting a response. //Update// I had a response from SP who foresee no issues with the legend consistency across the palette.
I’m hoping the idea has some legs but would love to hear your feedback either way. This is my first foray into set design so any pointers are not only appreciated but in fact hoped for.
Thanks for reading and I look forward to reading any further feedback!
///Updates///
So - I’ve updated the kits and generated a new form - if you filled out the first, please resubmit a new entry if you wouldn’t mind! Had 148 responses to form one - that’s definitely given me inspiration to keep iterating.
From the initial IC the major feedback was:

  • Ortho! I’ve added a kit as requested, feedback appreciated.

  • 2: Keep the Mono base but add a secondary legends kit - done!

  • 3: More colours and icon mods - my original tease a month or so ago included a number of tertiary colours to add flavour. I’ve incorporated these into the new Alchemist kit. The two novelty sets are designed as optional mix ins, however the combination of both can be used to cover 60% Ansi with icon mods as a bonus!

  • 4: Extra homing - Done!
    Keep the feedback coming and thanks to everyone that responded to R1, your feedback is the only way the set will improve, keep it coming! You’re all excellent.
    ///Updates 2///

  • From feedback I’ve reduced the text size on the modifiers and adjusted a few keys to remove overflow issues - they’re hanging together much more consistently now, updates reflected in kits below and updated within the Google form.

  • Again from feedback I’ve reworked the novelty kits, I’ve jazzed up the legends and flattened the colour scheme to match the overall aesthetic better.

  • Massdrop Form - I’m not intending to run this on Massdrop. I wouldn’t know where to begin with that anyway - this is just to help get visibility up.
