Peen Mount
Peen mount is a modified top mount with spacers in place of screws
In order to add durable threads into a 3d print, brass inserts need to be added into the case. While these are great in function, they can be quite difficult to install (an incorrect installation of just one insert requires the whole part to be reprinted). Using spacers in place of screws cuts the number of inserts used in one case from 14 to 6.
From my testing, I have found that peen mount feels and sounds exactly the same as top mount. While top mount is still a great mounting style, please do not purchase this keyboard expecting something different.


Designed for 3d Printing
The o60-1 was designed specifically for 3d printing.
In order to reduce the total time taken (and cost) to produce each unit, the boards are printed at a 0.32mm layer height. Everything along the Z axis is a multiple of 0.32 to ensure all parts are dimensionally accurate.
All visible or mating surfaces are parallel to the print bed to avoid stair stepping and uneven seams.
