Additional renders and 3D printed prototypes are forthcoming. Currently, here is an album with some preliminary pictures of what will be manufactured:
Campine mk. III album

The parts should clock in approximately 4 pounds, minus keyboard internals. The case can be assembled with both the acrylic diffuser and the steel bottom, or one or the other. As the images indicate, there will be the option to add our favorite feet to add an incline. They will be compatible with the vast majority of current PCBs and switchplates.
I am planning on powder coating the parts, to maintain a consistent finish between the aluminum and the steel. Powder-coating is a viable aesthetic option for both aluminum and steel parts. Here are some real world examples of colors and results.
Real-world examples

Im in the final leg of pricing, but at this point, the frame, diffuser, bottom and hardware should be under $150. Delivery in-hand is expected to be 10 weeks from GB conclusion. The parts will be manufactured, receive coloring post-treatment, be packed and dropped off for shipping all within a 60 mile radius. There will not be a cap on the order amount.
Im interested to hear color preferences (either pictured above or your own favorites), kit options (I can engage in bulk switch/PCB/stab purchases to lower the overall build price) or anything else you think would be important to include.
If anyone has any questions, please feel free to ask. Thanks!
