The Moiie Keypad

An Apple iie inspired keypad for the lovers of vintage mechs.

The Mo/iie/ came to me in a dream many moons ago. We see all these vintage/retro inspired keysets, full keyboards, cables…
But where are my macro/key pads?!
The Inspiration

My Vision of a Modern Revision (renders)





Currently Gathering interest for both 3D Printed and CNC Milled Units
After a few weeks on my desk, I absolutely love the idea of having this be a UNIT of Milled Metal on my desk as a classy keypad but Realistically, Im very very aware of what that would cost to mill. Not everyone is ready to shill out somewhere in the neighborhood of $150-$200 (estimate, depending on interest) for a keypad.
But none this less gathering interest. 3D Printed version GB/Stock run is likely to happen before any type of further, more serious mass production run begins to be looked into.
The Project Specs - Most are very subject to change.
 The PCB

  • /Gathering interest for compatibility options/ (MX/ALPS/CHOC, Etc.)
  • x3 Ind. Addressable RGB LED Indicators
  • x3 Spots for Rotary Encoder Positioning
  • USB Type-C
  • Fully Powered by QMK Firmware
  • Decent Layout Compatibility:

     The Case
  • 7° Steep Typing Angle
  • Floating Bed Styled Case
  • Inset Switch Beveling
  • 4mm Integrated Switch Plate (/*/Very Tentative/)
  • Two-Part Case, Accentuating the seam because lifes too short to be seamless.
  • No fills, just class
     VERY interested in looking into these features:
  • Integrated Cable. Some like this, some hate this, but I think it fits the theme very well and one less Cable to have to wear out I suppose lol
  • Brass weight. If we do CNC, it will already be expensive. So if we ball out…we are gonna BALL OUT.
  • Analog Sliders. This is iffy but I love the idea. QMK needs to have more clear support before this though.
  • Maybe Hotswap. I could see this happening
