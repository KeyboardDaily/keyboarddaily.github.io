The Colors

As with the previous version, this is still my inspiration, so don’t come at me about thai tea not being purple, cause there’s definitely purple in that pic.
After struggling to get Pantones to render as I was seeing them, I picked up a RAL book and decided to switch to that. While I do feel better about these, remember that all renders are an approximation and all of our screens can vary in how well colors are represented.
Alpha Base: RAL 075 90 20
Alpha Legend: RAL 050 50 60
Mod Base: RAL 350 20 15
Mod Legends: RAL 050 60 60
Accent Base: RAL 050 50 60
Accent Legend: RAL 350 20 15
Note that the alpha legends and the mod legends are two different shades of orange, the reasons for this were twofold, one it adds a bit more diversity in the oranges in the set, and it also helps with the contrast.
I set up a profile for my camera in lightroom using an xrite color checker, but I’m far from an expert. These are in no way guaranteed to be 100% color accurate, but should be used to give an added idea of what the color should be in addition to the renders

Renders
I did these renders with a variety of lighting, to hopefully give a rough idea of how these colors will fluctuate based on your particular desk setup.

As the premier beverage-themed keyset designer it’s my pleasure to introduce GMK Thai Tea.
What’s that? “But you just did a tea-based set with Taro?” Too bad! You’re not my supervisor!
After Taro R1, jacobsmirror came told me to do Thai Tea, but I said “no, that’s ugly” and never gave it a second thought. Until about 2 days ago, when I was like “oh yeah, I’ve been messing around with Fusion I guess I can finally mess with this without having to pay for someone else to render it”. And here we are.
