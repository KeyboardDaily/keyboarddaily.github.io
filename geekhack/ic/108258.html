What is it?
Hub16 is a macro pad created by the talented Josh Johnson, an Electronics Engineer from the land down under. As someone who is a proficient KiCad user and Electronics enthusiast at all times of the day, Josh wanted to design a macro pad with rotary encoders to adjust the trace and grid size in KiCad, along with enabling access to additional macros to improve his workflow. Following this, Josh added a USB hub to reduce the number of wires running across his desk, as a shorter cable could be run between the hub and primary keyboard.
At the time of writing, around 100 Hub16s have been sold with positive feedback from people all over the world. Hub16 is both QMK and VIA compatible.
Improvements
Whilst feedback has been overwhelmingly positive, a number of improvements have been made to the design which will ship with this GB.
-Spacing of the USB ports has been improved, enabling compatibility with an increased number of cables and adaptors.
-Improved USB hub design and layout, resulting in improved performance and robustness.
-Relocation of the reset switch for accessibility.
-A Kailh hotswap socket option will be available.
Notes on the USB Hub
The hub is a four port USB 2.0 Hub with Type-C connectors, aimed at allowing connection of keyboards, mice, memory sticks, wireless receivers, and other small devices (including a second macro pad!) to your computer.
Due to cost and space constraints, the hub has some limitations. The hub is not:
-USB 3.x, Power Delivery, Thunderbolt, DisplayPort, etc compatible.
-Designed to charge your phone quickly. Only 100mAis guaranteed per port.
-Designed to work with power hungry devices such as spinning hard drives.
-Guaranteed to provide full USB 2.0 speeds and performance on all ports.
-Suitable for use with long cables. Issues have been seen on cables longer than 2m.
Timeline
We are looking to have a relatively quick turn around on the group buy as the design is proven and final prototypes are in the works. Boards will be sourced from the same supplier as the earlier production run so not anticipating any issues.
The goal is to have all orders shipped within 10 weeks of the group buy finishing. A timeline will be confirmed when the GB opens following quoting from the PCBA and enclosure suppliers. Our biggest risks to on time delivery is delays in PCB assembly and international freight.
In an improvement from the original run, we are planning to ship from North America, the UK, and Australia. This should result in quicker and cheaper freight than shipping everything from Australia.
Pricing
We are aiming for pricing to be the same as the first run, at $70 USD + freight.
(As of 17th of August, $70 USD is £53 GBP, $97 AUD, and 59 EUR.)
A kit includes:
SMT Assembled PCB (encoders not soldered).
Acrylic sandwich case.
2 x Rotary encoders.
2 x Encoder knobs.
Final words
If this sounds like something you are interested in, please fill out the Interest Check so we can gauge interest and allow us to finalise pricing and timelines.
Have a great day.
