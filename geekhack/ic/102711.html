[/size] Hello everyone![size=78%]
So this has probably taken a bit too long, but I am finally updating this IC to better inform potential participants, and try and convey my plans for this board.
Right off the bat, let’s address the name change. I had several people note that V90 is also the name of a Volvo Estate car, and while Im sure its a great car, I thought it best to go with something else, after careful consideration and tossing many coins, I give you the Virgo, hopefully, the first of many boards from FLX <— Thats me .
[/size] The seed of an idea.[size=78%]
This board was originally an idea I had after seeing the EM7 and Alice type layouts, a layout that I have owned, and didnt really get along with, so I looked to change things to suit my own tastes.
This is the first Ergonomic keyboard I used, and I used it 8 hours a day 5 days a week for about 3 years. It was awful, but it was super comfortable, so comfortable that it took me a little while before I could go back to a standard layout.

So I started looking for a board that would suit my needs other than LZs Ergo, which is near impossible to source on the aftermarket, there was nothing else in the custom keyboard community that came close to filling this void for me. I set about designing it for myself, with a view that I would get one made for me and thats that. Three months later and after 13 iterations on the design, the Virgo came to be.

[/size] Testing out Geekhack.[size=78%]
Being proud of the board I had designed and with the encouragement of some friend in the community, I put my first, rather lacklustre interest check on here, to see if there was any appetite from anyone else for a board like this.
In 24 hours I had 200+ people register an interest, including some big names in the community who I respect and whos work I have admired for years.
I had to run it.
[/size] The Keyboard.[size=78%]

  • T6 6061 Aluminium Construction.

  • QMK compatible PCB

  • Gasket Esq plate mounting.

  • Seamless top housing over bottom housing design.

  • 7 degree typing angle

  • Weight system (Design to be finalised)

  • Aluminium Switch plate (Anodised)

  • USB-C Daughterboard (Opens sourced by AI03) Credits: Wilba, Hineybush, Xelus, Xondat, Aeryxz, Maximillian, AI03

  • 12 Degree split (12 degree improvement in radial deviation.
