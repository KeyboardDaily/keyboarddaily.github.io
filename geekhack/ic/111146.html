1.) INTRODUCTION About Kibou.
Kibou, 希望, is the Japanese noun for hope. It is a reflection of who we are: a bunch of hopefuls who believe that our passion for a better typing experience will allow us to make great products.
Why Harbour?
The keyboards name is a homage to Hong Kong - the place we grew up in. Historically, Hong Kong has long been an entrepôt between east and west - a place of trade, and, more importantly, a place where people of a common interest can gather. We hope Kibou will not only be the premier vendor for all things keyboard, but a community too.

4.) DESIGN
Approach.
Our design approach is straightforward: simple design and quality materials. We wanted to design a keyboard that we would want to use ourselves. From the mounting style to the typing angle, the keyboard is designed to deliver the best user experience possible.
Inspiration.
Hong Kong is home to more skyscrapers than any other city in the world. Our skyline is dotted with iconic buildings that helped author the aesthetic style of our keyboard. In particular, the textured layout on the sides was inspired by the BOC building. The numerous skyscrapers facing Victoria Harbour are characterised with smooth window surfaces supported by curtain walls and steel frames. Drawing inspiration from these visual elements, we decided to create a keyboard with a seamless design accompanied by a stainless steel weight.


We also drew ideas from the community. The visual appeal of Goks 7V design (the weight in particular) was a concept we wanted to explore and build from.
Mounting Method & Materials.
Using both community feedback and anecdotal experience, we believe that the softer typing feel that the 2mm Poron foam as a Gasket provides is more comfortable.
Assembly.
Aside from the seamless design, we also designed Harbour with the intention of making your assembly process as user-friendly as possible. This includes self-aligning slots between the top and botton case.

7.) FAQ Q: What is the weight of the board?
**A:**Around 2 kg.
Q: Will it be ISO compatible?
A: For our base kit, the PCB is ISO compatible, but unfortunately the plate is not. However, we will be releasing a universal plate file that will be compatible with ISO.
Q: Are stabilizers, switches, keycaps included?
A: Unfortunately, these are not included in the DIY kit.
Q: Will the plate files be released?
A: Yes, we will open source our plate files.
Q: When will the keyboards be shipped after the group buy?
**A:**Estimated arrival of around 7 to 10 months (Yet to be confirmed).
