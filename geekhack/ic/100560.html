Story:
I noticed dark low contrast sets like Ninja, and Black-on-Black, and said, ‘Hey, there’s no low contrast white set to put on my Polycarb keyboard!’ Then I thought to myself ‘What’s low contrast and white? … A Ghost’, and so the set was born.
Novelties:
For novelties, I wanted to do ‘word poetry’, where there are bunch of words that you can replace your alphas with and create little messages / poems. I see them as messages from the ‘Ghost’ in your keyboard. This is the reason I went for a flat profile (so you can put the words anywhere).
There are two sets, with mostly the same words: Ire Poetry, and Poetry. Ire Poetry uses a grisly font and blood red color for a more horror movie ghost. Poetry uses the normal font, but a bit darker, and a few less aggressive words.
Font:
In addition to Normal Alphas, St Croce Alphas offer a unique, stylized font. I chose this font because it gives you an optical illusion that letters are formed correctly, when in fact, they have the thin parts entirely missing. The way the characters are both ‘there’ and ‘not there’ fits the Ghost theme. The font itself is based on a worn away Roman font found in an old Franciscan church. Very fitting for a Ghost.

Profile: KAM
I set this up with no particular profile in mind (you can see discussion below). I have settled on KAM after I figured out novelties, kits, and just the overall price. The novelties (above) are best done with a flat profile, as you can move them around anywhere on the alpha block. If we had any sculpted profile, this would not be possible, as the amount of keys needed to make sentences on any row would be … um, difficult.
