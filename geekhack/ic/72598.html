eBay user nieva6173.89ejjb has been listing some really cool vintage computer stuff on eBay recently, and I won an auction of his for 13 IBM Model F and M boards. I’ll be going to pick those up soon, and he’s also listed a bunch of nice Apple keyboards. There seems to be a good amount of interest for these boards, so I figure I can run a GB for these since I’ll be going to pick up the IBM boards anyway!

12 of the AEK II boards are available, 11 of the ADK boards are available. 5 of the M0110 boards are available, 12 of the M0110A boards are available, and 12 of the M0116 boards are available, which brings the grand total to 52 keyboards.
I doubt these will fetch as high a price as the IBM lot did ($307), so the cost per keyboard should be pretty low, but of course we won’t know until we get to the end of the auction. You can order one of each board.
After the auction is won and I pick the boards up, I’ll begin sending out invoices. Of course, you’ll pay 1/5 of the total auction price if you want an M0110, and 1/12 of the total auction price if you want an AEK II, M0110A, or M0116. Right now, we’re going for a max of $85 for the ADK and M0110 lots and $240 each for the other lots, so that’s about $20 per keyboard before shipping except for the ADK’s, which will be about $8 before shipping. Shipping cost will depend upon your location and will be included in the invoice.
