The structure:

More

  1. We will be using daughter board for the USB port, to resolve the problem of USB port moving up and down caused by gasket mount system.
  2. Separate the RGB panel on the front to avoid light bleeding
  3. There will be a 3mm silicon dampening foam put between the plate and the PCB. The dampening foam will have extension
    parts on both front/back and left/right sides. With such extension parts on all sides, we could totally avoid the plate touching the case. In addition, it will be much easier to align the plate/foam/PCB into the top case, even you haven’t built any gasket keyboard before.
  4. PC middle frame is used to allow underglow to shine through sideway, and it’s also the bottom support for the gasket.
  5. The popular multi-color crown LOGO from E6.5 is back! If you look really carefully, the LOGO on E7-V2 consists of three parts, therefore, it has the ability to let underglow shine through so it can light up.
  6. Instead of o-ring gasket mount used on E8.5 and E6.5, we will be using rubber gasket on E7-V2. The main benefit using rubber gasket combined with leaf spring plate this time, is to provide excellent flexibility when typing (you have to try to know if you like this or not, after all, it’s all preference). But the downside of using rubber gasket is that we have to give up PC plate as it might not be suitable for this structure. In addition, we have to optimize and test the design of gasket mount on the arrow cluster with multiple iterations, all due to the principle that we stick to from beginning: original layout on slim bezel. We will do more testing to find a good balance on the trade-off, stay tuned!
    It’s definitely a challenge and difficult thing to never compromise on the design, as well as keep iterating and testing on details within the limited space that we give to this board. But I believe this will ultimately pay off, on the day when you receive this wonderful keyboard: E7-V2
