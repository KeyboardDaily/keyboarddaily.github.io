We are happy to introduce you to our brand-new design  Apes, inspired by the Planet of the Apes movie series.
The main character Caesar is an evolved chimpanzee who is the leader of the Ape Army and the adoptive son of human.
Caesar leads his fellow apes in a rebellion against abusive handlers and the police by unleashing the deadly ALZ-113 virus.
Caesar tries to seize dominance over the destiny of his own as well as the entire species.
The iconic scene where Caesar shoutout NO! in the first movie series was quite an impression for us!!

Getting back to the design, the beige color in the set is a representation of the bones and the sand coating of guns.
while the black and dark grey not only captures the biggest distinction between human and apes  the fur.
also matches the dark tone of the entire story.

4.Chimp Switches & Bullet Swithes
We would also like to introduce two switches come along with our theme.

both of which will be designed by Glove Studio, supervised by Domikey, and manufactured by Kailh.

The first one is Chimp Silent Linear Switch.
Just like the army of Champs sneaking into the base of enemies, silent, but powerful, waiting for the right moment to charge,
the Chimp switch uses Kailh linear silent V2 mold, and the Chimp Switch is the first one to utilized this mold in the market.

The second one is Bullet Clicky Switch.
Battle is on!! Machine guns crossing fire!!
Exactly what clicky switches sound like.
The Bullet switch uses Kailh Box Master mold, and the Chi Bullet Switch will be the first one to utilized this mold in the market.

5.Apes Puzzle

For us custom keeb fans, the keyboard is both a tool and artwork, some enthusiasts would even prefer to display the fully built keyboard than to actually use it.
Buy and sell in the mech market is very common but sometimes we would start to miss the keycaps/keyboards we sold.
What if there is an option for us to preserve the memory with a small, inexpensive art piece?
This is where the idea of Keycap Puzzle comes from.

I am a huge fan of art frame, and I would always frame some the small gadgets I love and hang them on the wall,

which inspired me to recreate my keycap design onto a thick paper board and make it detachable like stamps.


[Size of the puzzle] The final size of the puzzle is 1/2 of the size of the real keyboard keycap, which is A4 size cardboard
