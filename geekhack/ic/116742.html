The CLS-3//88 is the culmination of everything that Ive learned so far (achievements and stumbles alike), so there will be signigicant changes in design and also in the way that the product is offered to the public, with the one and only aim of bringing the best experience possible. DESIGN INSPIRATION
After CLS-2//65, Ive been contemplating ways to add some extra fanciness to my following product lineup, without sacrificing the elements of elegance and balance that are consistently instilled in my designs. The name of the game is how to harmonize the newly introduced curves with the stable imagery of the Ori bird flying towards the Sun (but not too close lol), and make the whole execution effortless.
Effortlessness and simplicity


OVERALL SPECIFICATIONS
Keyboard layout: F13 WKL
8-part assembly
Top and bottom cases: Alunimum
Internal weight: Brass
External weight: Brass/Stainless Steel
Switch plate:
Isolated gasket mounting
Typing angle: 6 degrees
Front height: 17.8mm
Custom PCB and Daughterboard by Mechlovin Studio
TECHNICAL CHANGES AND IMPROVEMENTS
Mounting and typing feel: I have adjusted the connection positions between the plate and the mount to reduce stiffness in some places.However, as soon as new prototypes are reevaluated. I’ll tune in one more time.
Sound: A realistic sound, reflected by a brass
Board assembly: My goal this time is with the assembly method where you will assemble the daughter board in the final stages. The aim is that the pcb will no longer be pinched by wires - affecting the feel of typing.
Packaging: Custom box - In the process of designing and testing
SALE INFO
For the first time ever and likely moving forward, Ori products will be offered through in-stock raffles. While doing traditional group buys makes things a lot simpler in terms of mobilization and logistics, it also puts a considerable amount of pressure on fulfillment, which in certain cases during my previous runs, affected my ability to meet expectations.
Keeping things in-stock, while having a huge financial strain on, allows me to work at my own pace, and most importantly, mitigates risks to my side instead of my customers.
PROGRESS UPDATE
The first unanodized prototype is already here, and my partners at Mechlovinand I are inspecting design and assembly elements, before moving towards building and sound testing.
