Story
Hi everyone, Im a keyboard enthusiast residing in Singapore!
Id like to introduce to you, the Loki65. It is a Full-CNC PC (Polycarbonate) board with the ability to shapeshift (we are looking at multiple mounting-styles).
I’m very excited to present this board to you here! I hope you will share this same excitement with me too. Lets get on with it!
The Name: Loki65
The inspiration came from the boards unique feature of having multiple mounting-styles, providing different typing experience and sounds based on how the user configures it.
This board is pretty much a shapeshifter, and I reckon you would agree too after looking through this post? (upcoming diagrams below)
Hence, our favorite Norse God, Loki came to mind and became part of this project. (Any Viking fans around? Norse Mythology?)
Research and Thought Process
As someone who prefers the sound of boards made of Polycarbonate over Aluminum, I wanted to design a board that encompasses Quality, Functionality, and that signature Polycarbonate THOCK. Besides, there seems to be quite a lack options for high-end PC custom keyboards in the market right now, compared to the Aluminum counterparts. Isn’t it?
Also, I’ve realized that many current Injection-Molded (IM) boards tend to have thin bezels or case “walls” which result in a fairly “hollow”-sounding and a less-than satisfactory typing experience.
Im definitely not an expert at materials, but Ive had my fair share of bad experience with other Polycarbonate keyboards in the current market. Theyve had manufacturing-defects, cracks, thread-shearing, squeaks, and a few more issues.
I cannot be sure if theyre of inferior quality, but it certainly goes against known material properties/characteristics of Polycarbonate for being extremely durable. The Loki65 will only be using the best-quality PC sheets from the fabricators.
We do agree, IM cases do have their own benefits, most-notably their lower costs due to bulk production. However, according to fabricators, CNC is sometimes the preferred choice due to higher precision and better quality-control. If you are seeking a high-end Polycarbonate custom keyboard, this is it.
To summarize, this is a board designed to achieve the following:

  1. Full-CNC PC, for maximum quality, durability and sturdiness. We’re talking about a thick, solid bezel and case. And to top it off, a hefty brass weight.
  2. Screw-holes with Stainless steel inserts to ensure absolutely no thread-shearing (PC material in screw thread does not wear-off).
  3. Ability to customize the typing and sound experience due to the plates unique design (featured below, in this post). Also, with an array of plate options available, the possibilities are huge.
  4. A high-end, premium keyboard.

Mounting System and Plate Design:
Now, lets talk about this. Much of the philosophy and design-concept of the Loki65 revolves around the unique structure of the plate. This was the core-focus during the creation process of the Loki65. I designed a plate that meticulously-integrated flex-cuts (leaf-spring) with mounting tabs on the edges, which then allowed a variety of mounting-styles to take place on a single plate.
The Loki65’s array of plate material options and mounting-styles enables the user to find the perfect plate flexibility. What will yours be?

The mounting-styles:
Mount 1 (top-mount)
The Plate is attached to the Top Case via M2 screws with Poron gasket strips and silicone O-Rings.

Mount 2 (gasket-mount)
The Plate is sandwiched between the Cases via Poron gasket strips.

Mount 3 (burger-mount)
The Plate is attached to the Top Case via M2 screws and silicone O-rings.

I am back with some updates here today!
Flexibility-Test
Quick Sound-Tests
My findings:
Typing on Gasket-mounting configuration gives a softer, fluffier feel, whereas Top-mounting gives a firmer, solid feel.
With leaf-spring activation (Top-Mounting), the plate is more flexible. At the flex-cuts, the plate is able to bend. During the tests, I am able to achieve a greater depression (vertical-distance travelled downwards is more than Gasket-mounting) of the plate and keys using this configuration.
With the Gasket-mounting configuration (NO spring-leaf activation), there is also a good degree of flexibility. However, the flexibility stems more from the compression of the gasket strips, and not so much from the bending of the plates. The depression of the plate and keys is lesser, compared to Top-mounting.

How’s everyone doing! It’s been awhile since I have last updated on the progress of the Loki65. Hope things are going fine for you. I have a few updates to share today:

  1. PC, and Brass Plate Flexibility Test:
    PC, and Brass Flexibility-Test
    My findings:
    Due to the current design of the plates, the PC plate is too soft to hold up the combined weight of PCB, switches and keycaps when using the Top-mounting configuration.
    There will be a new design implemented for the PC versions of plates. (Point 3 in this update)
    Both plates, when using the Gasket-mounting configuration (NO leaf-spring activation), gives that nice, soft feel; the Brass plate offering a slightly firmer touch to it. There is a good amount of flex on the board as seen in this demonstration.
    When the Brass plate uses the Top-mounting configuration (leaf-spring activates), there is also a good amount of flexibility. Stiffness of the Brass plate is greatly reduced due to the flex cuts. The design of the plate has worked as intended. Pretty happy with the results!
  2. Prototype V2:
    I have received the top and bottom cases of the Prototype V2. Based on the changes that I have made, the Loki65 feels a tad more solid than before. There are no longer any of the squeaks that I mentioned earlier on the previous update. Overall, the top and bottom cases feel a little heftier than the Prototype V1; there is a slight increase in the thickness of the bezels, and the thickness of the bottom case (on the interior). Here are some pictures of the changes, You will be able to see the side-by-side comparisons between the 2 different versions. This will be the final version, no more changes will be made.


  3. Polycarbonate plate
    Upon running numerous tests on the various plates, mounting-styles and setups, I have come to realise that the PC plate might be too soft for the Top-mounting and Burger-mounting configuration. This is largely-due to the size of the flex-cuts made for the leaf-springs. I will be reducing the size of these flex-cuts for the PC plate. The Brass and Aluminium plates will have their designs retained, while the PC plates will have a slightly different one.
  4. PCB
    There is good and bad news for this actually
    In the previous update, I have announced that the PCB will be designed by Coarse. However, due to certain unforeseen circumstances, our collaboration has ceased. I shall spare the details for now. Im rather disappointed at the unfortunate situation as well. This will add up to some delays.
    However, there is good news for me to share as well! I have managed to get in contact with Gondolindrim. I am very sure that many in the community know of him. He has been involved in numerous projects and collaborations. Thus, I am very pleased to announce that Gondolindrim and I will be collaborating for the upcoming PCB. I look forward to sharing more with you in the near future. Thank you for the patience and stay tuned for that! (:
