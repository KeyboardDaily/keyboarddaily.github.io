【IC】GMK Alchemy

“You are an alchemist; make gold of that.” - William Shakespeare
Much like a modern alchemist, the Alchemy colourway was first brought to us in DSA profile from our fellow UK brethren Chris Swires (famed for his two-tone table setup and a penchant for 40% boards), when he took a simple idea and created gold with it. When Chris reached out to us and gave us his blessing, we took advantage of this serendipitous opportunity to run our namesake keyset in GMK profile.
As we now propose to run this set in GMK profile, and cannot use SP’s colour codes exactly, we will be matching the actual SP chips with the closest alternative RAL colours.
The colourway itself is comprised of cool grey and beige tones representing base metals, a gold accent representing every alchemist’s primary goal, and warm brown legends representing the base element of earth to provide a stark contrast.
Quoting the designer himself: “The idea of the base combination is to provide a foreground backdrop to any board it’s placed upon, the subtlety of the base allows the board to be the star of the show rather than the set itself. It’s an accent piece, visual alchemy. The idea evolved from there.”
The proposed kits:
(Subject to interest - not all kits may run)
Base kit:

The base kit covers all of your standard layouts (ie 60%, HHKB, 65%, 75%, 1800 etc); while also including accent keys for esc, arrow and enters for both ANSI and UK ISO.
Apprentice kit:

With a colourway from Mr Swires himself, of course we had to include a kit for 40’s coverage. This kit includes the relevant keys to cover most 40% boards and also accents for the relevant keys.
Essence kit:

Standard spacebar kit, including all the shorty bars, an extra B key for Alice layouts, 6u bars with both standard & off-center stem and then accented 6.25u and 7u keys.
High alchemy kit:

A small but concise novelties kit consisting firstly of the ‘Ålç’ keys (first used as novelties in the DSA set), ‘Transmute’ keys for both ANSI and ISO enter and finally an R1 1u key with the Alchemist Keyboards logo. Each key comes in both the mod and accent colours.
