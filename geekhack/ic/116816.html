8Bit 60

I would like to share with you a board I have been working on for the past seven months - the 8-Bit 60
This board is inspired by popular portable gaming systems from the late 80s.

To pay homage to the console that inspired me I wanted to include features such as wireless connectivity, a working power switch, and a functional USB hub.
My goal was to recreate my childhood, while also making something new.

Pricing: Estimated pricing is around $489 USD
Specs
5˚ Typing angle
As for PCBs, Bluetooth (by Ella) will be a basic bluetooth PCB; Wired (by Sleepdealer) will offer a buzzer feature to be able to play 8-Bit tunes on start-up
Difference between BT and Wired

  • BT: no ISO, no Piezo buzzer, ZMK
  • Wired: Piezo buzzer, QMK/VIA/L
  • BOTH will support uses of the power switch, although it will differ depending on how you want to use it. Both will be able to use the usb hub (note BT can only use the usb hub when plugged in)

Whats next?

  • Finalize color selections (more on that in IC form); this will include the amount of Alu and poly option there will be.
  • I am currently thinking about using magnets to hold the top case and bottom case together, as opposed to case screws. I still need to add feet to the board, but I dont really like how the screw holes affect the placement. I want this board to have a very clean aesthetic and I feel the screw holes at the bottom get in the way.
  • I want to play around with bottom mount possibilities. I really want this board to be able to do both top and bottom mount, its quite easy to do but i just have to try it out and see if its really worth it.
    With many of my projects, I like to take time and get feedback and make changes. I also like to bounce ideas off of the community to see if we can make things even better. I have decided to create a design server to help with that, which will be used for following all my creations and ideas as they blossom
