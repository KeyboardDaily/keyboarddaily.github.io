About the set
JTK Sora is a set based off the colors of the sky, featuring a blue reminiscent of the sky for the alphas,
paired with an alternate sunset color for those who prefer the sunset instead.
The set is complimented with white modifiers being similar to the clouds we often see along with the sky,
along with a bronze-gold color representing the sun.
More context behind the set
More

My inspiration for this set came from staying mostly at home the past year because of COVID and
starting to miss the stuff I was able to do before outside under the sky.
Explanation behind the name:
The the name came from a Japanese friend of mine, Homiezoned who when I asked for help with naming it, named it Sora
because it meant “sky” in Japanese. I found it interesting because in Kanji, the same “sky” (空) character can also
mean “air or empty” in Chinese Hanzi and when combined with the character (天) in Chinese, it also means sky (天空).
Updates
Thank you everyone for all the support, set has finally entered GB!

More
Another important update regarding the set, JTK has just told us that the extra tab+backspace is
not possible for the Sunset alphas because of the way their molds are set up. We’ve unfortunately had
to modify the Sunset alphas kitting and remove the tab+backspace. Hopefully these are the final changes
needed.
On the bright side, we now have extra B in both base and the Sunset alphas, instead of spacebar kits.
JTK also has a new mold for the HHKB backspace, so we no longer have to use the ‘xbox’ one and
can use standard icon.
Other than that, we’re still waiting on the pricing/moq from JTK. Bachoo has also updated kit renders
to reflect the changes.

Colors
Pantone 278c, Pantone 1625c, Pantone 7407c, GMK WS2

More

WS2 was chosen over WS1 for the white because I felt WS2 was more similar to clouds than WS1, since WS1 has a bit of a cream tone to it.
As for why the blue chosen was 278c, originally I wanted something more like 2717c since the color reminded me of the sky the most but the contrast would be way too low for white legends.
278c also reminded of the sky but it was a bit deeper than 2717, making the contrast better with WS2.
The color 283c was also in consideration but GMK Oni already uses that color along with the same WS2 legends.
