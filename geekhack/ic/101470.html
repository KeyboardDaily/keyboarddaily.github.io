IRON165 by Smith + Rune

The IRON165 aims to be a distillation of these positive innovations, included in one premium board.

  • Optimized Plate, the Process, and FEA analysis*
    More

Keyboard plate FEA analysis was wanted to measure deflection and try to achieve a uniform plate deflection distribution across the keyboard. This concept has been done before with the Omega60 talking about mounting points. One of the first criticisms was that without a PCB and switch the study was worthless. I started out by modeling a small sample before moving to a full size plate. I constructed a small plate made of brass, switch made of POM, and PCB made of resin injected plastic.

I wanted to see the difference in displacement just a plate and a fully modeled with PCB, switches and Plate. With modeling of the entire system, there are two options in consideration:
Fully bonded - Solidworks treats the model as though it was fully welded together at every contact point.
No Penetration - Materials wont move through each other but can move independently from other models.
I estimated typing force to be around 2N max on each key as I measured my repeated tapping on a scale at 200g as the hardest I would ever really type (and I like to bottom out).

Next is the fully bonded model with plate and switches. Again a 2N force is applied to the top of the switch model with fixtures mounted at the four screw holes.

Lastly is the most realistic model the no penetration. Same force and Fixture as above. Notice how the displacement doesnt flow as uniformily through the switches vs the fully bonded model above. You’ll also see the displacement pattern is more similiar to that of just the plate.

If you compare the displacement results we conclude that just a plate can be used to model displacement without the extra PCB and switch modeling effort. The displacement of the plate model is approx 35% more than the plate with stiffness of PCB and Switches added to it.
Since each switch has to be setup and run individually I concentrated on making just the alphas as consistent as possible. This is still a work in progress, but here are the initial results.
This was the stock designed plate mounted with gaskets. Numbers in each switch represent the maximum deflection in millimeters for a 2N force:

Adding in relief cuts we can try to make the lower deflecting keys increase to the same deflection of the higher deflecting keys. This resulted in a decrease in standard deviation of 25% and a decrease in range by 35%.

FAQ
New studio? New board? Why should we trust you?
While this is a first in the keyboard community for us, we are proud of the team weve put together. Combined, we bring ten years mechanical engineering experience and five years of supply-chain management for small-batch, boutique products in other industries.
Smith + Rune is grounded in basic engineering principles, with a touch of magic and mysticism created by this wonderful community.
The rune logo is cool! Does it mean anything?
The two Norse runes featured in our logo, Sowilo and Raido, come from the Elder Futhark alphabet, the oldest form of runic alphabets. Sowilo means strength, power, success, while Raido roughly translates to ride, or journey. Together they form a bindrune, drawing the power of both into one creating a charm to ensure a successful endeavor and bringing a bit of luck to your desktop.
Why is it named IRON165? Wheres all the iron?
The iron industry essentially shaped the industrial revolution of the 1800s. Its ubiquity has led to its inclusion in nearly every industry. Iron is used to create many of the tools or machine parts we use today.
A keyboard is essentially a modern, but basic tool we use daily and we kept this central in our design choices. While beautiful, we placed function over form when designing the IRON165. In its simplest form, it had to be the best at being a tool first. Something durable, sturdy and a pleasure to use.
While not constructed entirely from iron (although steel is made with iron), we felt this board embodied the more abstract sense of the word. Iron is strong, iron is reliable, iron is long-lasting.
Whoa! Wheres the ISO support?
We will support ISO if enough interest is shown, but all of our plate files will be made open-source (including ISO) regardless. So even if youre an avid ISO user and we dont have an ISO plate, youll be able to get one made. We will also be optimizing our ISO plate for consistency, just like our other plates.
The PCB will support ISO out-of-the-box.
What parts will be user-replaceable?
Our hope is that everything, except the case itself will be user-replaceable in some form or fashion. One of our main goals when designing this board was to make absolutely sure that no IRON165 will ever become a glorified paperweight.
