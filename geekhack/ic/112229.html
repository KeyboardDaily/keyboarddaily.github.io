Hey everyone, we are two friends who are passionate keyboard hobbyists, and we also happen to be engineers. We started our company, 383 Boards, to bring our numerous years of product design and engineering expertise to the keyboard space.

With that, I present to you our very first board, the Whi65.
The biggest inspiration for this keyboard has been my Betta fish whose name this keyboard takes on, Whi. This fish, who refuses to die, has been with me for a very long time and has inspired the weight and badge designs. Whi was named after Kawhi Leonard in remembrance of him helping the Toronto Raptors win a NBA championship in 2019. Toronto has always been our home and we wanted to pay homage to the city and its basketball team, thus you will see subtle allusions to the Raptors logo as well as the hexagon corners of the board that represent the 6ix.

  • 1. DETAILS*

  • Isolated plate gasket mount

  • Sleek screw-less assembly

  • Typing angle: 7.0°

  • Front height: 19.5mm

  • Materials: 6061 Aluminum housings, Sandblasted brass weight, spacer, and badge

  • Mass: ~2.0kg (total), ~0.5kg (weight)

  • Plates: Aluminum & Polycarbonate (FR4 & Brass are being considered, TBD)

  • PCB: Mill-max hot-swap and soldering versions - configurable with QMK/VIA, ESD protection

  • Layout options:
    The soldering version of the PCB will support a variety of layout options: ANSI, ISO, split Left Shift, split Backspace, and stepped Caps Lock
    The hot-swap version will only support the ANSI layout.

Design Revisions from V1 prototypes:
The following design changes were made after using and testing out the V1 prototype board as a daily driver. These changes will be tested in the second round of prototypes:

  • Gasket strips have been made wider going from 2.5mm to 4mm to allow for easy application

  • The space between the PCB and bottom case has been increased from 1mm to 2.5mm, to give more room for the board to flex without bottoming out

  • Screws were added to the mid spacer to allow for an easier assembly

  • Switched from Phillips M1.2 screws to Torx M2 screws to give more durability

  • Plates were updated to add “leaf spring” type cuts around the edges to allow for easier flex

  • The weight’s mounting design also had to be updated to accommodate for the above changes
