Vector
Retro-Modern PCB Gasket 60% Keyboard

Blending the past with the present, Vector renews vintage keyboard elements with modern functions and stylings to create a distinctly retro board that blends into desk environments of the present.
Prototype Photo Gallery
For push notifications, please join either ai03’s Discord or the CannonKeys Newsletter.

A Leap Across Time
Large seams, tapered front ledges, rounded edges, large bezels, pen rails - Vector revives the elements of early keyboards and reshapes them to fit the modern.
Use the default penrail matched to the case for an undeniably retro feel; swap it for an accent color to create an entirely new impression that pairs well with vibrant keysets.

Bold Brushstrokes
An uniform visual seam journeys around the boards faces, defining the case parts and outlining the massive polished weight overlaid with accent laser engravings.
With large, blocky faces that wrap around the keyboards form with both a sense of roundness and overall sharpness, Vector blurs the lines between retro and current stylings.

Classic, Timeless Internals
Vectors homage to the past carries over to its internals with a plateless PCB-mount structure from early keyboards.
With full gasket isolation from the case and internal case screws, Vector brings the early inspiration to custom keyboard standards.

Specifications
Case: Anodized Aluminum
Weight: Polished PVDed steel, overlay laser engraving patternwork
Accent pieces: Penrail (default aluminum matched to case color, optional add-on accent colors)
Case Weight: 2.4kg
Case colors: TBD
Internals: Gasket-mounted PCB mount; internal screw; USB daughterboard
PCB: Solderable (non-hotswap), no flex slot
Plate: Optional FR4 plate included but not recommended
Vendors: CannonKeys
Price: TBA, but not pretty
Q&A
Q: No hotswap option?
A: Nope - the board is designed first and foremost as a PCB-mount structure which doesnt pair well with hotswap.
Q: Can I buy the board without weight engravings?
A: Nope - this is how the board is designed and envisioned.
Q: But I dislike how the weight accents look!
A: Then go find a different keyboard. Also, thats no longer a question.
Q: What do you mean by the price being not pretty?
A: The initial production cost quote we received blew past that of Andromeda. Even after revising the design to make it much more reasonable, its still not pretty.
Q: Why the lack of flex slots on the PCB?
A: Without a plate, it flexes so hard that it slams into the bottom of the case and short-circuits. Yes, we tried it - you can probably imagine how that prototype ended up.
Q: Whats the story behind the name?
A: As the winds of creativity moved the sands to form this design transcending the boundaries of time, their motion was captured within vectors of 
 Or not. I randomly slapped the name onto the design to refer to it before any name was chosen, and couldnt come up with anything else so it just kinda stuck until now.
Note
Early prototype revisions did not have a detachable penrail and had a flex-slotted PCB - some of the photographed units are of the old units and will differ from production.
