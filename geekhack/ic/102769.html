Gday and Welcome to GMK Port!
*Almost any person whos grown up in Australia over the last 90 years has probably seen an advert or heard the notorious jingle of Aeroplane jelly. A true Australian brand, Aeroplane was started in 1927 by Bert Appleroth, turning what was then a backyard business into one of Australias largest family-operated food manufacturers. Inspiration for this set was taken from one flavour in particular: port wine. The trifecta of colours corresponds directly with jelly slice, a common dessert to serve in Australias hot summer heat, with a combination of jelly topping, sweetened condensed milk, and crushed Arnotts Biscuits (another Australian brand).
*
You may notice this bears a striking resemblance to Ramas PORT U80-A; the colours have the same origin! /DISCLAIMER/ GMK Port as a set is not affiliated with RAMA WORKS.
This will be the first set I have both created and vendored, and has been a new challenge to get everything compiled on top of a full-time job as well as Ashkeebs starting up in the last few months. Thank you to everyone who has been patient who knew this project was being worked on, I can’t tell you how much I appreciate the support!

All colours chosen for the set are RAL Classic, and thus should be easier for GMK to replicate. I will be ensuring that the colours are as close to the renders as possible, and am hoping to order samples once the ball starts rolling.

The decision to move the numpad to a separate kit was, primarily, to keep the costs of the base kit down to as low as reasonably possible, while also promoting the sales of ISO enter keys for our European friends.
Thanks to inspiration from Dixie, I have decided to also use centered legends for the kit, as I personally prefer the aesthetic, and believe it may be a growing trend that he has started. I will still be going with the scooped/barred homing keys, though, as that is also personal preference.
