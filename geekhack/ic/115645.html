UPDATE 17/12/2021 AEST:
iLumkb has been added as the SG Vendor!
Inspired by the salt lakes found at Lake MacDonnell in South Australia, I present to you… well… ePBT SALT LAKES! This sets intention is to translate the wonderful colours of these lakes onto your keyboard, how good is that!
To achieve this, the custom keycap set will be made in a Cherry-profiled PBT plastic using a mix of dyesub and reverse-dyesub.

Colours
The green and pink Pantones represent the lakes within the inspiration picture at their most vibrant, while the orange/brown is the earth causeway that separates the two.
The white pantone then represents both the salt of the lakes and landscape which is concentrated within the area, and how the lakes become salt pans when there is particularly harsh drought in South Australia.
Pantone plastic chips will be used as the standard for colour matching within this set, though they have not arrived yet. This section will be updated as the IC progresses.
Note that the picture below only shows digital representations of the selected Pantones, and thus shouldn’t be taken as the final expectation for the set.

Background
More

The Pink Lake appears as it does due to it’s higher concentration of salt, allowing algae called dunaliella salina and bacteria called halobacteria to flourish. These algae and bacteria secrete carotenoid red pigments, the same found in orange and red fruit/vegetables, which gives the water its colour! The vibrancy of this colour is dependent on how diluted the salt content of the water is, as lower concentrations of salt in the lake inhibit the population size of the algae and bacteria, thus how much pigment can be produced.
The Green Lake doesn’t take on this pink colour due to it’s lower salt concentration as a product of larger water volume to the same amount of available salt. Therefore, it never achieves the pink of its neighbour. However, the somewhat green colour is due to chlorophyll-filled algae preferring in the lower salinity environment. Thus the intensity of the green can also fluctuate depending on the season and how favourable it is for these algae to grow!
Mind you, people notice the change in colour of the pink lake a lot more than the green lake because it’s, well, pink… which leads to the fun fact about the inspiration image!
You may have noticed that the colours appear a bit saturated, which is a keen catch . Funnily enough however, it is the land strip and the green lake that are arguably more vibrant than they appear in real life, not the pink lake! Go at the right time, and it indeed appears as in the image, which I was fortunate enough to have seen in person!
