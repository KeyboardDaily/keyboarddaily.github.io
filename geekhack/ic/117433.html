Hi all!
Welcome to the IC for PBT Maharaja.
This set is inspired by the Pink City of India, Jaipur–and in particular, the beautiful Hawa Mahal–and is a tribute to the opulence of the Rajput style of architecture.

Specifications
Manufacturer: TBD (see note below)
Profile: Cherry 1-1-2-3-4-4
Material: PBT
Technique: Reverse dye sublimation
Modifier Style: Traditional icon+text
Regarding the Manufacturer
I’ve requested samples from two manufacturers: KBM(KeyBee) and DCD(Drop). I will update this thread with pictures once the samples come in, and a decision will be made at that time. Currently, I’m expecting about a 1-2 month timeline for these.
IC Form & Social Media
As always, please do me a favor and fill out the IC Form below, as it really helps! If you’d like, you can also follow me and my other projects on Instagram.

Now onto the set!
Kits
Base

The base kit features support for 60% to TKL form factors and includes:

  • F13

  • 2u Shift

  • Terminal ISO support

  • Bottom row swaps for alternative operating systems

  • Full Alice support

  • 4-key 40s support

  • Numpad*

    Numpad and 1800 layout support can be found here.

  • Devanagari*

    The Devanagari kit offers a set of drop-in alpha replacements with Devanagari sublegends based on the InScript layout. A 2nd Tab has also been included to help cover another board in combination with the base kit.

  • Forties*

    The forties kit, in combination with the base kit, should provide support for most 40s/ortho layouts and includes accents.

  • Spacebars*

    The spacebar kit includes more niche spacebar sizes, as well as support for split-space layouts and accents for the most popular sizes.

  • Novelties*

    The novelties kit features ornamentation for your board based on architectural details of the Rajput style and includes:

  • ISO support

  • 40s support

  • Accent arrows

  • Deskmat*

  • Renders*

  • Phoenix by Cable Car Designs*

  • Vanquish65 by Geonworks*

  • Polaris by Ai03 and Kevinplus*

  • Acknowledgements*
    Special thanks to NoPunIn10Did for the help with the 40s kit and Manzel for the renders.
