Who are we?
ZTBoards is a two-man operation, and we are making our debut with the release of the AfterNoon keyboards. Individually, we are:
Ellicose- runner of Cheshire, Ilpse, and a few other small private group buys
PleaseBeMyFriend- builder for TypeMachina and JapaneseHorrorWriter (I tried to DM him once and he never replied tho).
ZTBoards (Zero Tolerance Boards) was an inside joke, but grew on us when we began to search for a name to use.
There is no origin behind the names of the boards, After and the Noon. We just wanted to pick the dumbest sounding name possible. Seriously though, at the time of creation we felt like there weren’t any boards publicly available that checked all the right boxes, so these two boards are a culmination of that. We’ve been working on these keyboards since before January of 2020; if it seems like this came out of left field, that’s because we wanted to have fully working prototypes and everything ready for production before posting an IC. Hopefully those who join this GB will appreciate these boards for what they are.
This GB is being run in collaboration with AEBoards.
Also: THESE ARE TWO DIFFERENT KEYBOARDS BEING RUN AT THE SAME TIME.

  • Other info:* Unfortunately, because of the encoder, the After will not officially offer VIA compatibility until official support is added for encoders. Both boards will be programmable through QMK. Regarding PCB thickness, we found that the 1.2mm was too flexible and we think that 1.6mm is a little stiff, but were still deciding and will have our decision by the time the group buy starts. There will also be no LED support, RGB or in-switch.
    We will be offering half plates with the kits because that’s how we intended the board to be used, but we will be opening plate files for public use.
    We are still sourcing and looking into other available knobs and the knob may change before the group buy begins.
    Regarding the knob on the After, please feel free to use your own knob of choice (it will still come with a knob either way). You want one with a shaft diameter of 6mm and a knob diameter of 19.05mm.

    There were only a few issues with our prototypes, none that affected use. We have made the necessary changes that will be reflected in the final product.
    For the Noon, the right side blocker will be placed properly. For the After, the top and bottom bezels will be slightly thinner and the side bezels have been widened to be proportionate to the now thinner bezels. We have increased the overall size of the internal weight as well for the After. Both boards will have bigger USB C daughterboard openings as well.
    Our monogram as well as AEBoards’ monograms will be engraved in the internal weight as reflected in the final renders.

  • Questions and Concerns:*

    Q: Hotswap PCB?
    A: No.

    A: We believe boards are a mostly subjective topic, so if you don’t like it that’s unfortunate. However, we have tried our best to make sure these boards live up to the price point and are going to be quality boards.

    A: Aluminum and other materials TBD.

    Q: Can you change the layout? I dont like upper right blockers.

    Q: Can you change the layout? I don’t like WKL blockers.
    A: No.
