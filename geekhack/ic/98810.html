What is KAT?
In the words of the zFrontier website:
Quote a brand new collection of high profile molds from Keyreative. Nineteen months of intense research, machining, and testing resulted in this delectably thick (1.658mm) sculpted design. Some inspiration also came from cats, as their claws provide an appealing slope to model keycap curves on.
Unlike SA, which can seem pointy and disproportionately tall on modern keyboards, KAT is smooth. Silky smooth. Superlatively smooth. Kat’s concave keycaps don’t stab or inhibit your fingertips during row jumps, nor do they have the gritty texture that one typically associates with PBT plastic. The characteristic dryness of PBT is present, but your fingertips won’t be ground into submission by the substrate.
Some keycap sets make generic nods to sound quality, but premium audio design was a serious consideration in KAT profile. The interior walls of its keycaps are angled such that sound is reflected in a precise pattern. That should, when coupled with high wall thickness, provide sharp clicks and muted clacks.
Finally, KAT keycap tops have the same dimensions across each row. That’s a vast visual improvement, but it also has a positive impact on muscle memory while touch typing.

Why KAT?
A flat profile just doesn’t feel right for this set I think, so the decision was down to manufacturers who can
do either doubleshot or reverse dye-sub. Frankly, I think a lot of us are tired of SA and GMK, and since
trying out new, innovative things is always awesome, KAT felt like a natural choice (plus: KAT -> cat, and cats are awesome).
Why specimen?
The name came to me out of pretty much nowhere, and it just stuck. This set feels pretty hard to name anyhow, so I’m happy I have one.
What kits are there?
Glad you asked:

The most basic of base kits. Should support pretty much all 60% to TKL keyboards and also HHKB style layouts. Two large spacebars are included so you don’t need to buy a spacebar kit for certain layouts anymore. Some common 65% layouts are also supported.

Lots and lots of extras, good 40% keyboard support should be included as well.

Full ortho support, hopefully a lot of different layouts are in here!

Vim movement keys along with some rather arbitrarily placed novelties.

A small and simple addon for people wishing to use a (UK) ISO layout.

A good splash of color for people wanting to spice up their TKL right hand side. Along with the classic numpad.

Just some blanks to cover some normal layouts, Colevrak, etc. keys aren’t supported, so this is also for you if that’s ok .

Northern layouts, many thanks to eskimojo of KAT Lich for the necessary keys

Southern layouts, Italy isn’t 100% supported through sheer stupidity on my part (you’ll need extras or deal with one wrong legend on the top left).

Hopefully everything a classic French layout needs.

For smaller, split spacebars.
