Lubestone

The Lubestone is our first lube station. It is made from Corian, and plays nicely with our standard size Workmat or as an accompaniment to our new Minimat. If you aren’t familiar with Corian, it is a solid surface countertop material that mimics stone in its weight and feel. The Lubestone will be dishwasher safe and durable for the foreseeable future.

If you aren’t familiar with our Workmat products, feel free to check them out on our website. They are hand cast from silicone rubber and are an excellent accessory for the keyboard building and other hobbies.
So. The Lubestone is made from half inch thick Corian material. This material is laser cut and polished to a glossy finish. The American Haptics logo is a pigmented epoxy inlay.

Production
Each Lubestone will be laser cut in our manufacturing facility and hand polished. The beauty of laser cutting is we have quite a bit of flexibility with the layout of the lube station. We can have multiple layouts for multiply workflows and preferences without changing the cost or manufacturing set up. A large part of this Interest Check is to gather enough variations in layout. Yet another variation possible is the color of the Corian. Corian comes in a galaxy of color schemes and treatments. However, we are limited to styles that do not have the very large mineral chunks. When the laser beam hits these stone chips, the beam refracts crazily within the corian material and makes a void, visible on the cut edge of the Lubestone. While some of this is likely unavoidable and acceptable, I believe it should be minimized for quality assurance and consistency.
Layouts
The current layout will be called standard. The interest check form will have a way for you to submit a layout. A handful of layouts will be chosen and become the launch styles available in the website
Colorway
To start, we will offer 2 Colors that cover basically a “light” and “dark” colorway. Corian is a little expensive, and for ever color that we add, we will need to pre sell about 20 Lubestones to cover the cost of investing in an entire sheet of Corian and having it cut to laserable sizes.
