Design and materials:
I was inspired to create this keyboard case while researching interior design trends, with terrazzo being one of the most prominent- terrazzo being the generic term for surfaces made with colored stone chips in a cement base.
I found out that I could make my own terrazzo items using a composite material called Jesmonite. It consists of a gypsum powder and a resin binder, that you can pigment and then pour into a mold. After it sets, it feels like engineered stone- think Corian or similar materials used in kitchen counter tops.
I thought this material would make an awesome keyboard case because (1) its heavy (2) you can make almost any color (3) I have lots of flexibility with regards to shape
I designed the Mason60 from scratch. I created a model of it using foam, then created a mold from that, then started casting prototypes. I chose 60% boards as a starting point as its the most standardized format. In the future, I will definitely look into other sizes (feel free to suggest a format with many people looking or aftermarket cases!).
In terms of design philosophy- I designed the shape of Mason60 to highlight the uniqueness of the material. I wanted a fat top bezel to show off the surface pattern and a chunky profile with very round edges which fit the “heavy” and “smooth” feel of stone or marble.
Surface finish and mechanical properties:
After casting, the case needs to be sanded down to reveal the chips inside. This is quite a laborious process, which I do with power tools for the rough pass, then finish by hand up to 2000 grit. The case is then sealed with a special wax, to provide water and stain resistance. The resulting finish is smooth enough to see reflections, like polished marble. Due to the casting process there may be some small air bubbles on the surface, the largest of which will be filled in, but small ones may remain. In my opinion the small bubbles add some character to the piece and makes it feel more stone like.
Bear in mind the hand-made nature of this case means it will not be dimensionally perfect like CNC aluminum, but this is part of the charm!
This material is fairly strong and tough, and shouldn’t shatter should you drop it on the floor(!). It will dent or scratch if dropped though, but not through normal usage. The finish can be repaired by sanding it down and re-applying the wax (I will include a small amount with each order). The final finish is water and stain resistant (water will bead on the surface, but shouldnt be left standing).
