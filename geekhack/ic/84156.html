Idea & Inspiration
I’ve been using the GMK CMYW mod pack for a month now, and just can’t get over how gorgeous the Magenta (MG1) looks. It’s easily my favorite color in GMK’s repertoire and I know of several others who feel the same. This led to me thinking - why not design an entire keyset around it?
I’ve decided on 2M as the secondary color to complement MG1. I feel that both colors play nicely off each other with just the right amount of contrast. Using pure white (WS1) did come into consideration, but I found the contrast a little too bright/too jarring while making comparisons, plus there is already an abundance of X-on-WS1 GMK sets on the market. The decision to go with an off-white color alongside MG1 also forms the basis for the set’s name.
Finally, I’m also toying with the idea of including a small accent kit (V4 on MG1) for that subtle-yet-distinct spark which also serves to enhance this set’s identity. I hope the accent kit will be well-received, but am open to tweaking it/swapping it out for other keys based on feedback from the community.

Key Features (subject to change)
 87 key ANSI base kit + 17 key numpad
 Text + Icon mods
 Bumped homing keys because scoops are prone to light bleed
 Winkeyless kit, currently includes two 1u R4 blanks which will probably get omitted to cut cost
 1800 adapter kit
 4-key accent kit (Esc, backspace, enter, caps lock)
Please comment below if you think additional features are necessary, but do bear in mind that the price GMK has quoted for this set in its current form is already a little on the high side. My current priority is making this set ISO compatible without it undergoing a drastic price increase.
GB Info/About Me
GMK requires a minimum order of 150 sets for this GB to come to fruition.
If the response to this IC is positive, I will be looking to run the GB on my own instead of partnering up with a vendor (purely in the interest of passing down as much savings as possible). I have experience in setting up/operating multiple webstores in the past and would be comfortable with creating one for this GB. In terms of operations and logistics, I am equipped to handle up to 250 orders but I highly doubt well reach anywhere near that figure.
For the best interests of the community, I shall try to implement a system where people can opt to pay a deposit instead of the full amount (similar to what Justnits did for his 9009 buy). The mechanics of this are still being fine-tuned as I’ll probably have to front some money out of my own pocket.
As for myself: I’ve been a mechanical keyboard user since 2013 and joined Geekhack in late-2015. I’m Malaysian and I moderate our local keyboard forum as well as the Malaysian MKB Facebook Group. I’m not sure what more to say here, but if theres anything else youd like to know about me, feel free to ask!
