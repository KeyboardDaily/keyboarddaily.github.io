Introduction
Hi, my name is DoctorNunu but most people probably know me as Boardrate as I haven’t been active at all on GH.
The Navigant TKL is a project that has been in the works for a couple of months now, I have always liked the HHKB layout and that control-less look.
lately I have been interested in TKL’s more and more but I still couldn’t let that HHKB layout go, and then it occured to me that I could create a TKL with that same look, and so the Navigant was born.
Combining two very popular layouts might sound good, but to some people this will be just weird as the HHKB is used for very minimal boards. But to me, this is the dream..
The Navigant will be a heavy board with a very sleek look, inspired by the CA66 and my own influences of sailing and being at sea. The Navigant speaks right from my heart…
This is my first ever design except for some homemade wooden cases, and I’m super excited to bring this idea to life with the help of you guys!
Renders

Badge
I have spent countless of hours trying to figure out what to do with the front logo, it started out as a brass badge but after discussing with some friendly users on Reddit I decided to let the badge go.
I replaced the badge with an engraving, unfortunately I couldn’t get a satisfying result with the font - so I’m considering bringing back the brass badge OR removing the logo completely from the front.
Input on this will be highly appreciated.

Technical information
 CNC Aluminum case - Will most likely be anodized unless the form results show different.
 Top mounted plate
 3.5 Degree typing angle
 QMK compatible PCB with USB C connector
 THICC Brass weight with anodized aluminium details

Estimated pricing

Development
Manufacturer status:
Manufacturer is confirmed for the case, prototype production will begin soon. The first prototype will be anodized black with a matte finish.
Waiting for confirmation from PCB manufacturer - Prototype will be produced as soon as I get confirmation.
Layout
The Layout is a HHKB inspired TKL layout but will support regular layouts aswell with the exception of keeping the “CTRL-Less design”.
