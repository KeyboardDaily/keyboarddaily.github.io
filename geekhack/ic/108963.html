    We are aware that the price is steep, however, we want to be transparent about it: Aluminum and brass prices are at an all-time high. This means that regardless of where this case is produced the starting price will already be high. We could have gone to China to manufacture the keyboard, but being European we wanted to not only support Salvun, as he is one of the best at his job but we wanted to make sure that we sourced everything we could from a European company. Furthermore, this is a very limited product and as you all know, limited runs of products are generally more expensive than unlimited ones. Both KEYGEM and Laminar want to make sure that we have worked diligently to try and keep the costs down so that it is accessible to everyone. This is a product that we both strongly believe in, and that we have thoroughly tested to ensure we provide the perfect experience. We decide to source the best possible components and are incredibly thankful to be able to have both Wilba and Salvun as parts of this amazing project. For those who think this is too expensive, we hear you and we just want to say, keep your eyes posted because we may have something for you in the future.

  • Updates on the Board:*
    In the original prototype, the gasket tabs were a little bit too far away from the outermost edges of the case, this caused some unevenness in the flexing of the board. To mitigate this the distance was reduced to 10 mm. The only other change was the removal of the middle tab underneath the spacebar. During testing, this proved to be beneficial in both acoustics and typing experience. Lastly, the compression ratio of the gaskets was reduced slightly. Initially, the gasket measured 2mm in thickness when uncompressed, and once assembled it was reduced to 1.25. For the final production run the initial thickness will be 1.5mm and the compressed thickness will be 1.25mm. This will provide a more soft typing feel and ease of assembly.
    Structurally, the screws were changed from M2.5 to M3 to help with the assembly of the board as well as to mitigate any possible marks. Salvun’s anodizer uses M3 screws to hang the case inside the baths, so using the screw holes ensures there will be no visible marks anywhere. Additionally, in the original design, all screws were the same size, this caused issues with the back screws since they were recessed heavily. To mitigate this, two different screw lengths will be used. This will also allow us to stylize the screws and make them a part of the design.
