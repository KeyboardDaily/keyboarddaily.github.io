1. DETAILS
The most notable feature and a key aspect of the design language of the board are the weights. Yes, there are two! There is a large bottom weight wearing the serpent engraving that the board draws the name from. Equally as prominent is the rear weight. One of my favorite aspects of the board is how the weight can be seen from top down when viewing and using the keyboard. The hint of brass or anodized aluminum wrapping over from the bottom all the way to the top really gives a unique look I feel hasnt been used often in custom keyboards.
The layout is another piece where I spent a lot of time before finalizing the end design. Many 65% keyboards have offset arrow keys now, but a sticking point for me has always been that the gap they are offset does not remain an equal 0.25u or 0.5u. Most boards will have the arrows offset 0.25u and the extra function keys 0.5u to accommodate a blocker or some other piece. With the Serpent65 - making this gap consistent and lending visual symmetry all the way around the function row and arrows was extremely important to me and I hope you enjoy the result! The board also supports ANSI bottom row, WKL bottom row with 7u and split BS.
Typing feel and sound are achieved by a carefully designed plate and gasket mounting system. I personally manufactured then tested numerous iterations of gasket materials, densities and sizes to find the perfect combination. The board feels great with multiple keycap profiles and sits at a 6.5° angle. A lot of care was taken in tuning the inside of the board to achieve optimal sound as well. A direct quote from MrPetrov himself Greatest sounding tactile board ever made! Trust me youll love how it feels and sounds. Typing tests, build guides, streams, etc etc etc will come before the GB so you have plenty of material to preview the board with.

2. CURRENT STATUS
The current iteration of the board will be ordered in a small quantity (25 units) in January. After those have been received the GB will open. Optimistically in Q1 but this may push to the beginning of Q2.
I have completed two successful rounds of prototyping. Its very important to me that I minimize all risk associated with the GB. I pay for all prototyping costs, PCBs, etc out of pocket and wont accept funds for a board that isnt able to be realized.
I understand that not all board can function this way and that sometimes the designer cannot afford this route, but its a personal belief of mine that ICs should have prototypes long before moving to GB. I have versions of the black, green and brass designs on hand and they all look amazing and you can see photos of them here before ever committing any money so you know exactly what you’re getting!
No design will ever be sold without first being prototyped and having it on hand.

4. FAQ
What’s included?
**Top and Bottom Case:**Anodized Aluminum (Black or Green available)
**Weights:**Brass, Anodized Aluminum (Black) and Limited Stainless Steel
**Plate:**One Plate (Aluminum, Carbon Fiber, Brass, POM, FR4)
**PCB:**Custom PCB by BlindAssassin with Unified Daughterboard
Price: $600 (starting+shipping)
**Timeline:**Q1 or Early Q2 GB
Split BS Support? Yes!
**Tsangan? ANSI?**Yes! The board supports the layout that is pictured above in the prototype - a bottom row of (1.5/1.5/7/1.5/1.5)
Additionally - for my winkey folks - it supports (1.25/1.25/1.25/6.25/1.5/1.5) Layout
**Hotswap?**Not at this time, but if it’s commonly requested in the IC form I am happy to revisit this.
**PC?**Again - not at this time.
**VIA?**Yes! And QMK of course. Open-source options are also being tested/ported.
